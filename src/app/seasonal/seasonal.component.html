Types of crops in India – DetailsKharif Crops.

  • July – October. Harvest – September to October.A.k.a Monsoon Crops.
  • Such crops require a lot of water Rabi Crops.
  • Sowing between October and November. Harvest – February to April. A.k.a Winter Season Crops.
  • Need cold weather for growth. ... Zaid Crop.

India has three major cropping seasons in a year.

  • Kharif-June-Early days of November-Paddy, Maize, Jute, Sugarcane, Cotton, Millet.
  • Rabi-November-March-Wheat, Tobacco.
  • Zaid-March-June-Fruits, Vegetables.

Which crops are grown in Kharif and Rabi seasons?

Rabi crops- wheat, barley, oats, gram, mustard, linseed. Kharif crops- rice, maize, millet, ragi, pulses, soybean, groundnut.

What are the crops grown in Zaid season?

  • The main crops include traditional summer crops like rice, corn, cucumber, melon, pepper, tomato
  • And some coarse cereals sown during the month of March and are generally harvested by June end.
